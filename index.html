The Freely Promise

Useful work without the bait-and-switch.

No surprise paywall

You should know the deal before you start. Freely tools do not let you do the work and then charge you to export it.

No accounts or fake trials

The tools are built for direct use. No login wall, no forced newsletter, no seven-day trial that becomes a subscription.

No watermarks

The result you create is yours. Downloads are not branded, degraded, or held hostage by a watermark.

No hidden uploads

When a tool can run in your browser, it should. If a future tool ever needs a server, that will be stated before you use it.

No redirect traps

Static outputs stay static. A QR code, file, image, invoice, or document should not become a subscription product by stealth.

No dark-pattern subscriptions

There is no hidden cancellation flow because there is no paid subscription flow. The tool either works freely or it does not ship.

Principles

How Freely tools should behave.

01

The export button is real.

Users should see how they get their final result before they invest time in the tool.

02

Local beats uploaded.

Browser APIs, WebAssembly, canvas, and client-side file handling are preferred whenever the job can reasonably run on the user's device.

03

Limits are stated early.

Large files, slow conversions, browser memory limits, and format caveats are product facts, not excuses for a paywall at the end.

04

Privacy claims stay specific.

Each tool should say what happens locally, what is stored, and whether anything ever leaves the browser.

Notes

Local-first does not mean magic.

Some jobs are heavy.

Video conversion, OCR, and large document processing can be slower in a browser than on a dedicated server. The tradeoff is that your files can stay on your device.

Some outputs need testing.

QR colors, transparent backgrounds, compressed videos, and document formats can depend on where they are used. Freely tools should help you spot those risks.

Free means complete.

Freely is not a teaser tier. A tool may be small, but the shipped workflow should be complete enough to finish the promised task.
